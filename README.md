# Virtual Glacier Tours

**Digital Interactive Excursions to Alpine High Mountain Landscapes and Research Sites**

## Objectives

Virtual Glacier Tours develops digital, interactive excursions to alpine high mountain landscapes and research sites of the Department of Geography at the University of Graz. The project makes current research on glaciers, geomorphological processes, and landscape change under climate change accessible to students, schools, and the general public through immersive virtual learning environments. It combines university research, digital innovation, and science communication.

## Relevance

Rapid changes in alpine landscapes — glacier retreat, rock glacier activity, increasing natural hazards, and shifts in vegetation and hydrology — are scientifically well documented but remain difficult to experience directly for most people. These processes occur in remote regions and are typically only observable through specialised field excursions. Virtual tours offer a way to make these landscapes digitally accessible and communicate their dynamics to a broad audience.

## Study Sites

- Dösen rock glacier
- Glacier forelands (various Austrian sites)
- Other alpine research sites of the University of Graz

## Methods

- High-resolution 360° panoramic photography using a portable 360° camera
- Aerial imagery and video sequences captured by drone (DJI Mavic Enterprise)
- Integration of panorama and aerial data into an interactive web-based platform
- Annotated virtual tours with supplementary texts, maps, explanatory graphics, and video sequences providing scientific background information
